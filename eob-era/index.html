The pain

Insurance payments arrive electronically, on paper, and in messy scanned documents.

Today

  • Staff opens paper EOBs, PDFs, or check stubs.
  • They search the patient and claim in the practice system.
  • They manually enter paid amount, deductible, write-off, patient responsibility, and remark codes.
  • They check if the check or EFT total matches the claim lines.

With PaperERA

  • Upload the EOB batch once.
  • The system reads each page and extracts the payment details.
  • Totals and claim matches are checked before anything is imported.
  • Open Dental receives an ERA-style file so staff can process inside their normal workflow.

Important safety framing

The AI is the reader, not the accountant.

This is the line we should repeat to dentists. The model reads the EOB. Rules and practice data decide whether it is safe.

Wrong claim matchNever auto-import when patient, date, code, amount, or payer match is weak.
Totals do not reconcileIf check total does not equal the claim-line payments, the batch becomes review-only.
Messy scanBad photos, missing pages, or unreadable text get flagged immediately.
Secondary insuranceComplex coordination of benefits starts as review-only.
Duplicate paymentCheck number, payer, amount, date, claim IDs, and file fingerprint are checked.
HIPAA/PHIReal version needs encryption, access logs, deletion policy, and vendors that support BAA.
Why Open Dental first

Open Dental gives us the cleanest first wedge.

Path A: native ERA workflow

Generate X12 835 text from the extracted EOB, create an ERA/Etrans record, then let Open Dental process it through its normal payment workflow.

Path B: direct posting later

After trust is proven, update claim procedures, claims, claim payments, and attach the EOB through the Open Dental API. More powerful, but higher risk.

Validation truth
If ERA import does not work reliably, the idea is weaker.

So the first technical proof is not a pretty app. It is one real EOB converted into a valid Open Dental-importable ERA workflow.

MVP plan

Build the smallest version that proves real office value.

Phase 0

Collect samples

20 to 100 anonymized EOBs/check stubs, top 5 payers, PMS version, clearinghouse, weekly volume.

Phase 1

Extraction and validation

Upload, OCR/AI extraction, strict structured data, total checks, confidence labels.

Phase 2

Open Dental ERA proof

Generate minimal valid 835, test in Open Dental, confirm matching behavior, find missing identifiers.

Not promising full autoposting yet. The validation target is faster, safer ERA preparation for real EOBs.
Questions for your brother-in-law

We need office reality, not guesses.

1. What practice management software do you use? If Open Dental, which version?
2. Do you already receive ERA/835 files from any payers?
3. Which clearinghouse do you use? DentalXChange, ClaimConnect, Change Healthcare, Vyne, Tesia, other?
4. What are the top 5 insurance payers by EOB volume?
5. How many EOBs or checks per week, and how many pages per batch?
6. How many staff hours per week go to payment posting?
7. Do checks usually cover one patient or many patients?
8. Can you provide 20 to 100 anonymized real samples?
Decision we want from the call

Is this painful enough that a dental office would test it with real documents?

If yes, next step is not a big build. Next step is a controlled proof with anonymized samples and one Open Dental workflow test.

Green light

Pain is real, volume is meaningful, samples are available.

Yellow light

Pain exists, but PMS or payer workflow makes ERA import unclear.

Red light

Volume is low, existing ERA coverage already solves it, or staff would not trust it.
