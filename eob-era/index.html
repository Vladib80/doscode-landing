The office problem

Insurance payments often still require manual posting from EOBs, PDFs, and check stubs.

Typical process today

  • Staff opens paper EOBs, PDFs, or check stubs.
  • They search the patient and claim in the practice system.
  • They manually enter paid amount, deductible, write-off, patient responsibility, and remark codes.
  • They check if the check or EFT total matches the claim lines.

Possible process with PaperERA

  • Upload the EOB batch once.
  • The system reads each page and extracts the payment details.
  • Totals and claim matches are checked before anything is imported.
  • Open Dental receives an ERA-style file so staff can process inside their normal workflow.

Important safety framing

The AI reads the EOB. The system checks the numbers.

The product should not blindly post payments. It reads the EOB, checks totals and matching signals, then shows what is safe to process.

Wrong claim matchNever auto-import when patient, date, code, amount, or payer match is weak.
Totals do not reconcileIf check total does not equal the claim-line payments, the batch becomes review-only.
Messy scanBad photos, missing pages, or unreadable text get flagged immediately.
Secondary insuranceComplex coordination of benefits starts as review-only.
Duplicate paymentCheck number, payer, amount, date, claim IDs, and file fingerprint are checked.
HIPAA/PHIReal version needs encryption, access logs, deletion policy, and vendors that support BAA.
Why this starts with Open Dental

Open Dental looks like the best first system to test.

Option A: use the normal ERA workflow

Convert the EOB into an ERA-style file, then let Open Dental handle it through the payment workflow staff already knows.

Option B: direct posting later

Later, after trust is proven, the product could post more directly. That is more powerful, but higher risk.

Main thing to test
The first proof is whether real EOBs can become reliable Open Dental ERA imports.

Before building a full app, the key test is one real anonymized EOB converted into a valid Open Dental-importable workflow.

Suggested pilot plan

Start small and prove whether this helps your office.

Phase 0

Use real examples

A small set of anonymized EOBs/check stubs, top payers, software version, clearinghouse, and weekly volume.

Phase 1

Read and verify

Upload files, extract payment details, check totals, and label each item clearly.

Phase 2

Test Open Dental import

Generate a minimal valid ERA 835, test it in Open Dental, and see what information is needed for reliable matching.

This is not promising full autoposting on day one. The first goal is faster, safer ERA preparation for real EOBs.
Questions for you

The most useful feedback is how this fits your real office workflow.

2. Do you already receive ERA/835 files from any payers?

4. What are the top 5 insurance payers by EOB volume?
5. How many EOBs or checks per week, and how many pages per batch?
6. Roughly how many staff hours per week go to insurance payment posting?
7. Do checks usually cover one patient or many patients?
8. Can you provide 20 to 100 anonymized real samples?
